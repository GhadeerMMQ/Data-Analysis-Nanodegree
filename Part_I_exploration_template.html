Introduction

This dataset contains U.S. domestic commercial flight records from January 2025, sourced from the Bureau of Transportation Statistics (BTS) On-Time Performance database. Each row in the dataset represents an individual flight, including information about scheduled and actual arrival times, delays, flight distance, and operational characteristics.

The dataset was limited to a single month to maintain a manageable size while still capturing a large and diverse set of flight records. This allows for meaningful exploration of flight delay patterns without introducing seasonal variation across multiple months.

The primary focus of this analysis is arrival delay, which serves as an indicator of flight punctuality and passenger experience. Supporting features such as flight distance, scheduled departure time, origin airport, and derived delay status are used to explore how arrival delays vary across different operational contexts.

The goal of this investigation is to understand general delay patterns using exploratory data visualization, progressing from univariate analysis to bivariate and multivariate relationships.

What is the structure of your dataset?

The dataset consists of individual U.S. commercial flight records from January 2025, where each row represents a single flight. The dataset contains several hundred thousand observations and includes both numerical and categorical variables. Numerical variables include arrival and departure delays, flight distance, and scheduled times, while categorical variables include airline, origin airport, and destination airport.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?

The primary feature of interest in this dataset is arrival delay, which measures how late or early a flight arrives relative to its scheduled arrival time. Arrival delay is a key indicator of flight performance and passenger experience, making it a meaningful outcome variable for this analysis.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?

Several additional features are used to help investigate arrival delays. Airline and airport variables, such as the reporting airline and origin airport, allow for comparisons in delay performance across different carriers and locations. Scheduled departure time is used to examine whether flights departing at certain times of day are more prone to delays. Flight distance is included to explore whether longer flights tend to experience greater delays. Together, these variables provide context for understanding patterns and differences in arrival delays.

    delay

    .size()
    .reset_index(name='Count')
)

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 5))

    x='OP_UNIQUE_CARRIER',
    y='Count',
    hue='DelayStatus'
)

plt.title('On-Time vs Delayed Flights by Airline (January 2025)')
plt.xticks(rotation=45)

Conclusions

This investigation explored U.S. flight performance in January 2025 with a focus on arrival delays. Initial univariate analysis showed that most flights arrived on time or slightly early, while a smaller proportion experienced substantial delays, resulting in a right-skewed delay distribution. This established arrival delay as a meaningful feature for further analysis.

Bivariate and multivariate explorations revealed that arrival delays varied across operational contexts. Delay severity differed by categorical groupings such as delay status and origin-related characteristics, while flight distance showed little direct relationship with arrival delay magnitude. Scatter plots and box plots highlighted that both short- and long-distance flights can experience significant delays, indicating that distance alone is not a strong predictor of arrival performance.

Multivariate analysis strengthened these findings by examining how multiple features interact. When delay status was considered alongside arrival delay and flight distance, severe delays were shown to be concentrated within delayed flights across all distances. These interactions suggest that arrival delays are influenced by multiple contributing factors rather than any single explanatory variable.

Overall, the step-by-step exploratory approachfrom univariate to multivariate analysishelped uncover patterns, validate earlier observations, and avoid premature conclusions. While this analysis was limited to a single month of data, it provided valuable insight into flight delay behavior and demonstrated how thoughtful visualization supports effective data-driven investigation.
